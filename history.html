History

Early History

The Indiana Territory was split out of the Northwest Territory in the year 1800. Indiana was founded in 1816 The State Capital was the Louisville-neighboring City of Corydon, Indiana (Indiana Historical Society, 2024). Indianapolis was chosen as the State's Capital in 1825, due to its central location and intersection of the White River.

Present Day

Indiana is well-known for its engineering colleges, such as Purdue and Rose-Hulman University. IU Health, Kroger, Eli Lilly and Cummins are some big name corportaions that provide hundred of thousands of Indiana jobs. The current Indiana unemployment rate is 3.5%, which is 0.4% lower than the nationwide average. Indiana is home to 6.86 million people in 2023 (STATS Indiana, 2024).
